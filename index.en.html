Stunfest

Co-organizing the "Indie" section

Stunfest, a video game culture festival in Rennes created in 2005 and organized by the association 3 Hit Combo, has hosted us since 2012 to coordinate a part of the festival.

Initially focused on independent game studios, this section has expanded year after year to now include original installations, temporary workshops, and other performances offered during the festival.

The aim of this section is to showcase video game creation in all its forms, whether from France or elsewhere. Furthermore, a jury awards several prizes to the "favorite" games from the Indie Zone.

A Game Jam, the Stunjam, is also offered to the public before the festival. Its aim is to provide a jam for enthusiasts (or not) of video game creation, but also to showcase games created by amateurs at Stunfest on a dedicated stand.

The association is also responsible for setting up the website showcasing indie exhibitors, organizing and running the public voting process, and producing the trailer for the games presented at the Indie Awards.

Global Game Jam

Organizing the event

Global Game Jam (GGJ) is an annual global workshop that invites team-based game creation over 48 hours, in video game, board game, or hybrid formats. A theme is presented to the jammers at the start of the 48-hour creation period. Furthermore, numerous optional achievements can be unlocked in each game to increase the challenge.

Initially co-organized in Rennes by the association 3 Hit Combo , Global Game Jam has been entirely organized by Indie Collective since 2021.

Our role in this event lies particularly in its overall management, presentation and smooth running throughout the weekend.

Stunjam

Organizing the event

Stunjam is a 48-hour team game creation workshop, in video game, board game, or hybrid format, which usually takes place the weekend before Stunfest, a video game culture festival in Rennes. Stunfest

A theme is presented to the jammers at the beginning of the 48-hour creation period, and optional achievements can be completed on each of the games to spice up the difficulty of the creation.

The unique aspect of this jam session is its integration into the festival. The projects are showcased at Stunfest on a dedicated stand alongside independent studios.

Our role in this event lies particularly in its overall management, presentation and smooth running throughout the weekend.

Octojam

Organizing the event

Octojam is a workshop that offers team-based game creation over 48 hours, in video game, board game, or hybrid format, taking place on the last weekend of October. A theme is presented to the jammers at the start of the 48-hour creation period. Furthermore, numerous optional achievements can be unlocked for each game to add a challenge.

This jam is unique in that it takes place during the daylight saving time change. Jammers are therefore offered the option of participating in the Octojam's 0h game jam , a one-hour workshop that takes place between 2 a.m. (summer time) and 2 a.m. (winter time).

Our role in this event lies particularly in its overall management, presentation and smooth running throughout the weekend.
